Getting started

TRON energy is a network resource consumed by smart contracts and USDT transfers. Instead of burning 14+ TRX per transaction, you can rent energy from specialized platforms for as little as 1.2 TRX. The rent lasts from 15 minutes to 30 days depending on the service. Energy delegating reduces your transaction fees by up to 90% compared to burning TRX directly. No staking, no lock-up — just pay and use.
We scan 20+ platforms every cycle — reading their SUN rates, API prices, and on-page TRX quotes. The board sorts platforms by 65k energy for 1 hour (the most popular format) and highlights the best deals. Use the energy calculator above to check different amounts and durations. Green values mean below average, blue means the absolute cheapest. Try different combinations — sometimes 15-minute bursts are cheaper than full hour rentals.
A standard USDT TRC20 transfer requires ~65,000 energy. Without renting, the TRON network burns about 14 TRX. With rented energy, you pay approximately 1–3 TRX for the same transfer — saving 80–90%. At 130k energy (for contracts or wallets without USDT), expect 2–6 TRX. The exact price fluctuates as platforms adjust their SUN rates based on demand and pool utilization.
Each platform sets its own SUN rate (the internal price unit) based on energy pool size, demand, and operating costs. Marketplaces like TronMax use bid/ask order books. Fixed-rate services like FeeSaver and NETTS have stable SUN rates that change less frequently. API-based platforms like Feee.io and iTRX offer tiered pricing with quantity discounts. This natural competition creates price spreads of 3–5× between the cheapest and most expensive option — exactly why comparison exists.
