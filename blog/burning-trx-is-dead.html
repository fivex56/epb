Burning TRX Is Dead: Rent Energy and Keep Your Money

Kai Zhen · DeFi Writer · June 7, 2026 · 5 min read

Burning 14 TRX every time you send USDT is boomer behavior. With energy rental you pay 1.2–3 TRX for the same transaction and keep the rest. Here's the math behind why renting beats burning — always.

The numbers don't lie

A standard USDT TRC20 transfer consumes ~65,000 energy. If you don't have staked energy, the TRON network burns TRX to cover the cost. Current burn rate: ~14 TRX per transfer. At $0.20/TRX, that's $2.80 per send. Do 10 transfers a day, that's $28/day — $840/month — just on fees.

With rented energy at 2.3 TRX (current cheapest on Energy Price Board): same transfer costs 2.3 TRX ($0.46). That's an 84% savings. 10 transfers: $4.60/day instead of $28. You save $700/month. The math is brutal.

Why staking 3000 TRX for "free" energy is actually worse

Yes, you can stake TRX to get free energy. But staking 3000 TRX locks up $600 indefinitely. Meanwhile, renting 65k energy costs 2.3 TRX per use. How many transfers before renting costs more than staking? 3000 / 2.3 = 1,304 transfers. If you do 10 transfers/day, that's 130 days. But here's the catch: during those 130 days, your staked TRX could have earned yield elsewhere, and your capital is locked. Renting keeps your capital liquid.

When to still burn TRX

If you're sending one transaction this month and don't want to learn about energy rental — burn the 14 TRX and move on. For everyone else: rent. The user experience has matured. Telegram bots make it one-click. APIs make it automatic. TRON hit 300M accounts in May, USDT supply crossed $60B, and fee pools are richer than ever. There's never been a better time to switch.
